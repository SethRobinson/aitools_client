SET_PROMPT1: (A single flowing paragraph describing a 20-second video scene. This can span multiple lines. Include these elements:

*** CHARACTER DESCRIPTIONS — MANDATORY FOR EVERY SCENE ***
The video AI has NO MEMORY. Every single SET_PROMPT1 MUST introduce every character with a FULL physical description, even if they appeared in previous scenes. Include ALL of these for EACH person:
- Age (specific, e.g. "in his 40s")
- Ethnicity/skin tone
- Build (lanky, stocky, petite, broad-shouldered, etc.)
- Hair (color, length, style)
- Clothing (specific items and colors)
- One distinguishing feature (scar, tattoo, glasses, beard, etc.)

Use the EXACT SAME description for each character every time so they look consistent across scenes. Do NOT use character names — always refer to them by their visual description.

GOOD: "A wiry man in his 40s with a graying beard, weathered tan skin, and a red flannel shirt rolled to the elbows"
BAD: "The man" or "he" without first establishing who he is visually
BAD: "Marcus" or any character name

THEN 20 seconds of sequenced action, structured as three beats:
- BEAT 1 (~7 seconds): Opening action
- BEAT 2 (~7 seconds): Escalation or complication
- BEAT 3 (~6 seconds): Payoff or transition

For each beat:
- Physical actions and gestures in sequence using "then", "as", "suddenly"
- Dialog: include 3-5 short spoken lines spread across the scene, each ONE sentence max. Always put a physical acting direction between every dialog line. Never have two lines of dialog back-to-back without action between them.
- Show emotion through the BODY, not labels. No parenthetical mood words like (angry) or (sad).

Dialog format: The [visual description], [physical action]: "[Short line.]"
GOOD: The tall woman with short red hair grabs his arm, pulling him back: "We can't go that way."
BAD: SARAH (worried): "We can't go that way. It's too dangerous and we'll get caught."

Include 1-2 camera directions at natural transition points.
End with one sentence describing the audio/sound environment.

CRITICAL RULES:
- EVERY person in EVERY scene MUST have a full visual description: age, ethnicity, build, hair, clothing, distinguishing feature. NEVER say just "the man" or "the woman" without these details.
- Use the SAME character descriptions word-for-word across scenes for consistency.
- NEVER use character names. Always use visual descriptions.
- NO parenthetical emotion labels. Show it through the body.
- Every scene MUST include dialog — 3-5 short spoken lines with physical action between them.
- Balance action and dialog.
- Be LITERAL and CLEAR, not poetic or vague.
- Describe what the camera SEES, not feelings or abstract concepts.
- Do NOT use the word "cinematic".
- ~250-350 words for SET_PROMPT1 to properly fill 20 seconds.
- Each scene should advance the story — don't repeat the same situation.
