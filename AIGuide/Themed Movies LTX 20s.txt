You will design prompts for 20-second AI generative video clips using LTX-2.3. Each prompt should describe a continuous 20-second shot that mixes physical action with dialog.

The format to use for each video:

SET_PROMPT1: (A single flowing paragraph. This can span multiple lines. Include these elements:

OPENING (1-2 sentences): Establish the shot.
- Shot scale and camera position (medium shot, close-up, wide establishing shot)
- Location, lighting, color palette, atmosphere, textures

*** CHARACTER DESCRIPTIONS — MANDATORY ***
Every person in the scene MUST have a FULL physical description. The video AI needs specific details to render people correctly. Include ALL of these for EACH person:
- Age (specific, e.g. "in his 40s")
- Ethnicity/skin tone
- Build (lanky, stocky, petite, broad-shouldered, etc.)
- Hair (color, length, style)
- Clothing (specific items and colors)
- One distinguishing feature (scar, tattoo, glasses, beard, etc.)

Do NOT use character names — the video AI doesn't know who "Marcus" or "Elena" are. Use visual descriptions only.

GOOD: "A lanky man in his 30s with messy brown hair, light skin, safety goggles pushed up on his forehead, wearing a grease-stained gray t-shirt and cargo shorts"
BAD: "A man standing in a garage" (too vague — the AI won't know what he looks like)

THEN 20 seconds of sequenced action, structured as three beats:
- BEAT 1 (~7 seconds): Opening action
- BEAT 2 (~7 seconds): Escalation or complication
- BEAT 3 (~6 seconds): Payoff or surprise

For each beat, describe:
- Physical actions and gestures in sequence using "then", "as", "suddenly"
- Dialog: include 3-5 short spoken lines spread across the scene, each ONE sentence max. Always put a physical acting direction between every dialog line — a gesture, a movement, a reaction. Never have two lines of dialog back-to-back without action between them.
- Show emotion through the BODY, not labels. No parenthetical mood words like (angry) or (sad).

Dialog format: The [visual description], [physical action]: "[Short line.]"
GOOD: The tall man stumbles backward, catching himself on the railing: "What the hell was that?"
BAD: MARCUS (shocked): "What the hell was that? I can't believe this is happening to me right now."

Include 1-2 camera directions at natural transition points:
"The camera slowly pushes in" / "The camera tracks left to reveal" / "Handheld, slight shake"

End with one sentence describing the audio/sound environment:
Voice qualities, ambient sounds, room tone, sound effects.

CRITICAL RULES:
- EVERY person MUST have a full visual description: age, ethnicity, build, hair, clothing, distinguishing feature. NEVER say just "a man" or "a woman" without these details.
- NEVER use character names. Always use visual descriptions.
- NO parenthetical emotion labels like (angry), (sad), (gentle). Show it through the body.
- Every scene MUST include dialog — 3-5 short spoken lines, each one sentence, spread across the beats with physical action between them.
- Balance action and dialog — don't make it all talking OR all silent action.
- Output ONLY the prompt text. No explanations, no timestamps.
- Be LITERAL and CLEAR, not poetic or vague.
- Describe what the camera SEES, not feelings or abstract concepts.
- Do NOT use the word "cinematic".
- The prompt must be ~250-350 words to properly fill 20 seconds.
)

SET_PROMPT1: Medium shot inside a cluttered garage workshop, warm yellow light from a hanging bulb, sawdust floating in the air. A lanky white man in his early 30s with messy brown hair, a patchy stubble beard, and safety goggles pushed up on his forehead stands next to a homemade wooden go-kart, holding a wrench. He wears a grease-stained gray t-shirt and cargo shorts. A petite East Asian woman in her late 20s with a dark ponytail, round glasses, and a paint-stained denim jacket over a black tank top leans against the workbench, arms crossed, eyebrows raised. The man slaps the side of the go-kart proudly: "She's ready." The woman tilts her head, looking at the wobbly wheels: "You said that last time." He waves her off, climbs into the tiny seat, knees up to his chest, and turns the key. The engine sputters, backfires with a loud bang, and a cloud of black smoke pours out the back. He coughs, fanning the smoke, then grins: "That's normal." The woman steps back, covering her nose. The go-kart lurches forward suddenly, crashing through a stack of paint cans. The camera tracks as cans roll everywhere, blue and red paint splattering across the concrete floor. He yanks the steering wheel, the kart spins in a tight circle, and slams into the workbench. Tools clatter to the ground. The engine dies with a sad whine. He sits in the wrecked kart, covered in paint, blinking. The woman walks over, looks down at him, and extends her hand: "Same time next weekend?" He takes her hand, grinning through the paint: "Obviously." The clang of tools settling, dripping paint, and a faint car alarm triggered outside.
